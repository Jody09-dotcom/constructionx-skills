# CDM 2015 Duty-Holders and F10 Notifiability

Reference for the cdm-2015-check skill. Summarises the Construction (Design and Management) Regulations 2015 duty-holders and F10 notifiability test. Authoritative source is HSE L153, "Managing health and safety in construction".

## Duty-Holders

| Role | Who appoints | Key duties | Trigger |
|------|--------------|------------|---------|
| **Client** | n/a (is the person or org for whom the project is done) | Make suitable arrangements for managing the project. Provide Pre-Construction Information. Appoint Principal Designer and Principal Contractor in writing when more than one contractor. Ensure adequate time and resource. Ensure welfare is provided. | Always. |
| **Principal Designer (PD)** | Client, in writing | Plan, manage, monitor, and coordinate health and safety in the pre-construction phase. Prepare PCI. Coordinate with PC during any design work during construction. | More than one contractor on site. |
| **Principal Contractor (PC)** | Client, in writing | Plan, manage, monitor, and coordinate health and safety in the construction phase. Produce the Construction Phase Plan. Manage site rules, induction, welfare. | More than one contractor on site. |
| **Designers** | Self or by client | Eliminate foreseeable risks during design. Reduce what cannot be eliminated. Provide information about residual risks to the PD/PC. Includes temporary works designers. | Anyone preparing or modifying a design. |
| **Contractors** | Self or by client/PC | Plan, manage, monitor their own work. Provide information and training to their workers. Produce RAMS. Comply with site rules. | Anyone managing or carrying out construction work. |
| **Workers** | n/a | Take reasonable care. Cooperate with duty-holders. Report risks. | All operatives on site. |

## Single vs Multi-Contractor

- **Single contractor** (one contractor only): PD and PC are not required. The sole contractor takes on the CPP-producing role.
- **More than one contractor**: PD and PC appointments are required. Client must make the appointment in writing before the construction phase begins. If not made, the client takes on those duties by default.

## Domestic Clients

A domestic client is someone having construction work done on their own home (not for business). Under CDM 2015, the domestic client's duties pass to:

- The **sole contractor** (for single-contractor projects), or
- The **Principal Contractor** (for multi-contractor projects), or
- The **Principal Designer** by written agreement (for multi-contractor projects, if agreed).

Domestic clients are not expected to manage the CDM process. The contractor takes it on.

## F10 Notifiability Test

Under CDM 2015 Regulation 6, a project is notifiable to HSE if **either** of the following is met:

1. Construction work is expected to last longer than **30 working days AND have more than 20 workers working simultaneously** on site at any point during the project, OR
2. Construction work is expected to **exceed 500 person-days**.

If either threshold is met, the client (or PD/PC on their behalf) submits Form F10 to HSE before the construction phase begins.

F10 submission: https://notifications.hse.gov.uk/fod/

Note: notification is a legal duty of the client. Non-notification of a notifiable project is a breach of CDM 2015 Regulation 6.

## What an F10 Contains

- Project location
- Client details
- Principal Designer details
- Principal Contractor details
- Projected start date
- Projected duration
- Estimated maximum workforce
- Estimated total workers

## Good Practice Reminders

- Appoint PD and PC in writing before the construction phase starts. A contract clause does not count unless it names the role and the organisation.
- Keep a copy of the PCI sent to contractors. The client can't provide "reasonable information" after the fact.
- Review the CPP before work starts. A CPP that lives in a drawer is not a plan.
- For domestic clients, the contractor should write a letter or short email explaining the CDM pass-through. Sets expectations and provides a paper trail.
