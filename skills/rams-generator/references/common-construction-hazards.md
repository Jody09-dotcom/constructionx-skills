# Common Construction Hazards

Hazard library for UK construction RAMS drafting. Not exhaustive. The competent-person reviewer adds site-specific hazards.

## Working at Height

**Triggers:** Any work at 2m or more, or where a person could fall and be injured at any height. Ladders, scaffolds, MEWPs, roof work, edge working, leading edges.

**Standard controls:**
- Eliminate: redesign to work at ground level where possible
- Substitute: MEWP or tower scaffold instead of ladder
- Engineering: edge protection, guardrails, netting, kick boards
- Administrative: Work at Height Regulations 2005 compliance, rescue plan, inspection regime (scaffold Scaftag weekly), competent users (PASMA for towers, IPAF for MEWPs)
- PPE: harness and lanyard with suitable anchor, helmet with chinstrap

**Regs:** Work at Height Regulations 2005.

## Manual Handling

**Triggers:** Lifting, carrying, pushing, pulling of loads. Blocks, lintels, radiators, materials handling.

**Standard controls:**
- Eliminate: mechanical lifting (telehandler, crane, pallet truck)
- Substitute: smaller/lighter unit sizes (e.g. aircrete vs dense block where structurally suitable)
- Engineering: block grabs, suction lifters, mechanical aids
- Administrative: team lifts, training (Manual Handling Operations Regulations), rest breaks
- PPE: gloves (grip, cut), back support where appropriate

**Regs:** Manual Handling Operations Regulations 1992.

## Noise

**Triggers:** Breakers, saws, power tools, plant. Any task above 80dB(A) triggers assessment, above 85dB(A) triggers mandatory hearing protection.

**Standard controls:**
- Eliminate: choose quieter method
- Substitute: quieter tool or technique
- Engineering: damped tools, acoustic enclosures
- Administrative: rotation, exposure limit monitoring, exclusion zones
- PPE: hearing protection (plugs, muffs), rated to reduce exposure below 85dB(A)

**Regs:** Control of Noise at Work Regulations 2005.

## Dust (incl. Silica)

**Triggers:** Cutting, grinding, drilling, breaking concrete, brick, block, stone, mortar. RCS (respirable crystalline silica) is the big one.

**Standard controls:**
- Eliminate: offsite fabrication to avoid on-site cutting
- Substitute: wet cutting, low-dust products
- Engineering: on-tool extraction (M-class vacuum), water suppression, local exhaust ventilation
- Administrative: exposure monitoring, health surveillance, task rotation
- PPE: FFP3 mask (tight-fit, fit-tested), eye protection

**Regs:** COSHH 2002. HSE Workplace Exposure Limits EH40.

## Hand-Arm Vibration (HAVS)

**Triggers:** Breakers, sanders, grinders, impact wrenches, any vibrating tool in prolonged use.

**Standard controls:**
- Eliminate: method change
- Substitute: low-vibration tools (check manufacturer data)
- Engineering: anti-vibration grips
- Administrative: trigger-time monitoring, Exposure Action Value and Exposure Limit Value calculations, task rotation, health surveillance
- PPE: anti-vibration gloves (limited protective effect, warmth more than dampening)

**Regs:** Control of Vibration at Work Regulations 2005.

## Struck-By and Moving Plant

**Triggers:** Plant movement, reversing, pedestrian/plant interaction, loads falling from height, materials falling from scaffold.

**Standard controls:**
- Eliminate: separate pedestrian and plant routes
- Substitute: plant with 360 camera, proximity alarms
- Engineering: exclusion zones, banksmen, tower crane landing zones, mesh guarding
- Administrative: Traffic Management Plan, induction, signage, permits
- PPE: high-vis (class 2 min, class 3 near plant), helmet

## Electricity

**Triggers:** Temporary site supplies, buried cables, overhead lines, use of 230V tools.

**Standard controls:**
- Eliminate: 110V CTE power only on site
- Substitute: battery-powered tools
- Engineering: RCDs on every supply, service drawings, cable avoidance tool (CAT scan + Genny), overhead goalposts
- Administrative: permit to dig, permit for live work (avoid), qualified electrician for isolations
- PPE: insulated gloves (for live-adjacent only)

**Regs:** Electricity at Work Regulations 1989. HSG47 (avoiding danger from underground services). GS6 (overhead lines).

## Asbestos

**Triggers:** Any work on a building built before 2000. Demolition, refurb, services installation, drilling into walls or ceilings.

**Standard controls:**
- Eliminate: avoid disturbing the material
- Substitute: where ACMs are present, consider a different route
- Engineering: enclosure, negative pressure, on-tool extraction
- Administrative: Asbestos Survey (Management or Refurbishment/Demolition), UKATA/IATP training, licensed contractor for higher-risk work, Asbestos Register on site
- PPE: FFP3 RPE, disposable coveralls

**Regs:** Control of Asbestos Regulations 2012.

## Confined Spaces

**Triggers:** Manholes, chambers, tanks, pits, deep trenches over 1.2m, enclosed voids.

**Standard controls:**
- Eliminate: work from outside the space
- Substitute: remote method
- Engineering: ventilation, gas monitoring, tripod and winch rescue kit
- Administrative: Confined Space Permit, top-person, rescue plan, training
- PPE: escape set, harness

**Regs:** Confined Spaces Regulations 1997.

## Hot Works

**Triggers:** Welding, cutting, grinding, hot-air stripping, torch-applied roofing.

**Standard controls:**
- Eliminate: cold-method alternative (mechanical fixings, solvent-free)
- Engineering: fire blankets, spark arresters, fire watch
- Administrative: Hot Works Permit, 60-minute fire watch after completion, isolate smoke detection where agreed
- PPE: welding PPE, fire-resistant overalls, face shield

**Regs:** Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005.

## Slips, Trips and Falls on the Level

**Triggers:** Untidy site, trailing leads, wet floors, unfinished surfaces, poor lighting.

**Standard controls:**
- Administrative: daily housekeeping, clear walkways, lighting inspection, cable covers
- PPE: grippy footwear

**Regs:** Workplace (Health, Safety and Welfare) Regulations 1992.

## Lone Working

**Triggers:** Single operative out of sight or out of earshot of others.

**Standard controls:**
- Administrative: check-in procedure, lone-worker device, buddy system, restricted hours
- PPE: n/a
